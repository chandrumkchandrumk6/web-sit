Studying Abroad Across africa

Author: Mouctar 05-09-2021

Muktar.Panafrican Student
Share
Follow @MuktarBalde

Going abroad for studies is one of the most exciting experiences that every young man and woman dreams to pass through once in his life. But when you ask for advice about where to study, ninety per cent of the answers will point to European, American, Asian and Australian Universities. Only a few people will direct you to Africa including, Africans themselves. Yet, mama-Africa , has indeed many opportunities to offer its sons and daughters.

In 2020, I was awarded a scholarship from the African Union - AU to study at one of its Pan-African Universities located at Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology (PAUSTI), in Kenya-the country of safari.

I was extremely excited as I knew travelling itself is a holistic experience no matter where you're going. The challenges were rising left and right since I had shifted from my comfort zone. Language barriers and cultural diversity were waiting for me to welcome me in my new environment. Once registered, I started learning English, the language I was dreaming of speaking years back. After five weeks of the intense course, I was able to express myself yet, many things had to be done but remember Rome was not built in a day, and learning a language takes time. At the Pan-African University for Basic Sciences, Technology and Innovation, the environment eases things more than they seem to be.

Having friends from all over the continent with all kinds of backgrounds motivates you to keep pushing. It's Africa represented by a University. Apart from learning a new language, studying abroad especially, in Africa, has many advantages:

  • Cultural mix
    • The African culture in its diversity varies from one country to another or even within the same country. Places such as Pausti offers you the chance to interact with more than forty nationalities. You will, of course, enrich your African nature.
  • Gaining a global perspective
    • Studying abroad helps you develop a well-organized mindset and widen your view of other people's cultures. Whichever subject you're taking, it enables you to think from an international angle and allows you to face worldwide challenges and propose innovative solutions.

  • Lifetime Experience
    • If you get the chance to study in East Africa in general and Kenya in particular, then the safari is for you. Kenya is a fascinating, touristy country. It has many national parks where you could see all kinds of wild animals.

On the other hand, despite the benefits, there are some disadvantages:

  • Feeling homesickness
    • Wherever you go, home remains home! you will miss your family, friends and colleagues.
  • Higher Risk

    • Often those who go to study abroad are young men and women who might be less mature to face all the challenges associated with travelling. Parents might be worried about your security.

There are many benefits and drawbacks related to studying abroad. However, no matter where you want to go to study, Let's come back and build the Africa we want, the Africa of knowledge and Power, as it was millenniums ago with civilisations such as ancient Egypt, Kush, Axum, etc. If you're more curious about studying abroad, check out the references below.
